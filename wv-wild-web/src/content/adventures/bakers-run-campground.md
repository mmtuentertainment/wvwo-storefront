# Bakers Run Campground

A quieter alternative on Sutton Lake with 77 sites including coveted waterfront locations. Electric hookups available at most sites, with 12 non-electric options for tent campers wanting a more rustic experience.

## What Makes It Special

- **77 campsites** - smaller and quieter than Gerald R. Freeman
- **30 waterfront sites** - wake up to lake views
- **Electric hookups** (30/50 amp) at 65 sites
- **Boat ramp on-site** - direct lake access
- **Hot showers** in modern bathhouses
- **Playground, basketball, and volleyball courts**

## Campsites

| Type | Sites | Nightly Fee |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Electric Waterfront (30/50 Amp) | 30 | $28-$34 |
| Electric Standard (30/50 Amp) | 35 | $24-$30 |
| Non-Electric | 12 | $18-$22 |

**Season:** May 1 - September 30 (shorter than Freeman)
**Reservations:** Recreation.gov
**Recreation.gov ID:** 10122583

## Amenities

- Bathhouses with hot showers
- Flush toilets
- Dump station
- Playground
- Boat ramp
- Basketball and volleyball courts
- Fire rings and picnic tables

## Critical Information

**SHORTER SEASON** - Bakers Run closes September 30, while Gerald R. Freeman stays open through November. Plan accordingly for fall trips.

**CELL SERVICE IS LIMITED** - Download offline maps before arrival.

**Quiet Hours:** 10 PM - 6 AM
**Stay Limit:** 14 days per 30-day period
**Pets:** Allowed on leash

## Why Choose Bakers Run?

- **Waterfront sites** - Book early for prime lake views
- **Quieter atmosphere** - Less crowded than Gerald R. Freeman
- **Good for fishing** - Boat ramp puts you right on the water
- **Family-friendly** - Playground and sports courts for kids
