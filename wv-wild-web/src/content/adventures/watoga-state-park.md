# Watoga State Park - WV's Flagship Resort Park

Watoga State Park is West Virginia's largest and oldest state park - 10,100 acres established in 1937. This is a full-service resort park with lodge, restaurant, 18-hole championship golf course, and 33 rental cabins.

## What Makes It Special

- **Largest WV state park** - 10,100 acres in Pocahontas County mountains
- **Historic Watoga Lake Lodge** - 30 rooms with full-service restaurant
- **18-hole championship golf** - Par 72, 6,200 yards, April-October
- **Brooks Memorial Arboretum** - 350+ tree species with paved accessible trails
- **33 rental cabins** - Standard, deluxe, pet-friendly, and ADA accessible
- **88-site campground** - Full hookups, showers, laundry
- **Olympic-size pool** - Memorial Day through Labor Day

## Activities

- **Hiking** - 32 miles of trails from easy arboretum walks to backcountry routes
- **Fishing** - Trout and bass in Watoga Lake and streams (WV license required)
- **Swimming** - Olympic-size pool with lifeguards
- **Golf** - 18-hole championship course with cart and club rentals
- **Wildlife Viewing** - Black bears, deer, wild turkey, 200+ bird species
- **Ranger Programs** - Arboretum walks, wildlife programs, fly fishing workshops

## Brooks Memorial Arboretum

The arboretum is a highlight - 350+ tree species on paved, accessible trails. Daily guided walks at 10 AM from May through October. Free admission.
