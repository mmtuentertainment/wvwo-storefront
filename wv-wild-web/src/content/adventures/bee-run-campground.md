# Bee Run Campground

Twelve primitive tent sites on the quiet eastern shore of Sutton Lake. No electric. No water hookups. No showers. No reservations needed. Just pull in, pick a site, pay at the self-service station, and camp.

## What Makes It Special

- **12 primitive sites** - tent camping only
- **First-come, first-served** - no reservations, no phone calls
- **$10-14/night** - cheapest camping on Sutton Lake
- **Vault toilets** - the only amenity
- **Boat ramp nearby** at Bee Run Day Use Area
- **Solitude** - while others pack the big campgrounds, you have peace

## What You Get

Each site includes:
- Picnic table
- Fire ring
- Lantern post

That's it. This is primitive camping.

## What You DON'T Get

- **NO electric hookups**
- **NO water hookups**
- **NO potable water** - BRING YOUR OWN (5+ gallons recommended)
- **NO showers**
- **NO dump station**
- **NO reservations** - show up and hope for a spot

## Fees & Policies

**Nightly Fee:** $10-$14 (cash or check at self-service station)
**Season:** Late May - Early December
**Stay Limit:** 14 days per 30-day period
**Pets:** Allowed on leash

**BRING YOUR OWN DRINKING WATER** - This cannot be emphasized enough. There is no potable water at this campground.

**Pack it in, pack it out** - No trash service. Leave no trace.

## Essential Gear

- [ ] Drinking water (5+ gallons minimum)
- [ ] Tent and ground tarp
- [ ] Sleeping bag rated for cool nights
- [ ] Camping stove and fuel
- [ ] Cooler with ice (no electricity)
- [ ] Headlamp with extra batteries
- [ ] Firewood or gather deadfall
- [ ] Fire starter and matches
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Trash bags (pack it out)
- [ ] Offline maps (limited cell service)
- [ ] Cash for self-service fee station

## Nearby Activities

- **Bee Run Day Use Area** - Adjacent boat ramp with Sutton Lake access
- **Sutton Lake fishing** - Walleye, bass, crappie, catfish
- **Swimming** - Bee Run Day Use Area (no lifeguards)
- **Bakers Run Campground** - 8 miles away if you need amenities
- **Gerald R. Freeman Campground** - 10 miles away for full hookups

## Getting There

From I-79 Exit 62 (Sutton/Gassaway):
1. Head north on WV-4 toward Sutton Dam
2. Turn right onto Bee Run Road
3. Follow signs to Bee Run Day Use Area and Campground
4. About 45 minutes from our shop

## Who Is This For?

Bee Run is for campers who want:
- Quiet over convenience
- Low cost over amenities
- Nature over neighbors
- Self-sufficiency over services

If you need electric, showers, or reservations, choose Gerald R. Freeman or Bakers Run instead. Bee Run is for people who like their camping simple.

Stop by the shop for camping supplies, fishing licenses, and a fill-up on water before you head out.
