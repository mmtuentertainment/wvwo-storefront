# Elk River WMA - 18,396 Acres of Central WV Hunting

Elk River Wildlife Management Area spans nearly 19,000 acres of mature hardwood forest in the heart of Braxton County. Steep hills, ridges, and benches dominated by oak-hickory forest with old-growth timber interspersed throughout.

Two major rivers - the Elk and Holly - flow through, with Sutton Lake at the heart. One of West Virginia's oldest and most accessible WMAs.

## What Makes It Special

- **18,396 acres** of public hunting land
- **Class Q accessible** - One of 14 WV WMAs with expanded vehicle access for disabled hunters
- **30+ miles of trails** throughout the WMA
- **2 shooting ranges** - 100-yard and 175-yard for sighting in
- **Sutton Lake access** - Camping, boat ramps, and fishing nearby
- **Year-round access** - No gates or seasonal closures

## What to Hunt

| Species | Season | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| White-tailed Deer | Sept 27 - Dec 31 (archery) | 14" minimum antler spread |
| Wild Turkey | April 20 - May 24 (spring) | 2 bearded birds |
| Black Bear | Sept - Dec (check dates) | BG + Bear Damage stamps required |
| Ruffed Grouse | Oct 18 - Feb 28 | Daily limit: 4 |
| Gray Squirrel | Sept 10 - Feb 28 | Daily limit: 6 |

## Critical Regulations

- **WV hunting license required** with Class BG stamp for big game
- **Electronic game check** within 24 hours via WVhunt.com
- **400+ square inches blaze orange** during firearms deer season
- **No Sunday hunting** in West Virginia
- **No ginseng harvesting** - ILLEGAL on WMA lands ($500-$2,000 penalty)
- **Portable tree stands only** - Must be removed after each hunt

## Getting There

From our shop in Birch River:
1. Head north on WV-4 toward Sutton
2. Take I-79 to Exit 67 (Flatwoods)
3. Follow WV Route 19/40 south to WMA access
4. About 60 minutes total drive time

Stop by for hunting licenses, stamps, ammo, and local intel on what's moving.

## Kim's Take

"One of the most accessible WMAs in our area - good trail network, Class Q access, and Sutton Lake right there for camping. Just remember: no camping on WMA land itself, but the Corps campgrounds at Sutton Lake are first-rate. And please - no ginseng hunting here. It's illegal on public land."
