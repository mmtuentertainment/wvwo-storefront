## Sutton Lake Family Activities

### Picnic Areas and Shelters

**Total Shelters:** Five picnic shelters located in two day-use areas[^1][^2]

**Shelter Locations and Details:**

#### **Downstream Day Use Area (Shelters 1 \& 2)**

- **Location:** Below the 210-foot Sutton Dam, overlooking Elk River[^3][^2]
- **Number of Shelters:** 2
- **Capacity:** Each shelter accommodates 6-8 picnic tables[^2][^3]
- **Amenities per Shelter:** Water and electric hookups, large charcoal grill[^3][^2]
- **Area Features:** Playground, fishing pier, small boat access, horseshoe pits, cornhole boards, large parking area, accessible flush toilets[^2][^3]
- **ADA Accessible:** Shelter 1 is ADA accessible[^3][^2]


#### **Bee Run Day Use Area (Shelters 3, 4 \& 5)**

- **Location:** 1734 Bee Run Road, Sutton, WV 26601[^2][^3]
- **Number of Shelters:** 3 (Shelters 3, 4, and 5)
- **Views:** Overlook Sutton Lake as it winds 14 miles up the Elk River valley[^3][^2]
- **Capacity:** Each shelter accommodates 6-8 picnic tables[^2][^3]
- **Amenities per Shelter:** Water and electric hookups, large charcoal grill[^3][^2]
- **Area Features:** Large swimming beach, playground, Sutton Lake Marina nearby (rentals and concessions), flush toilets, extensive parking[^4][^2][^3]
- **ADA Accessible:** Shelter 4 is ADA accessible (note: no direct ADA access to restroom facilities)[^2]


#### **South Abutment Day Use Area (Behind Dam)**

- **Number of Shelters:** 1 shelter[^1]
- **Area Features:** Swimming beach, picnic area[^1]
- **Details:** Limited information available

**Shelter Reservation System:**

- Shelters available first-come, first-served unless reserved in advance[^1]
- Reservations: Call 1-877-444-6777 or visit Recreation.gov[^1][^2]
- **Reservation Fee:** \$50-\$60 per day[^5][^1][^2]
- **Season:** Closed for winter; typically reopen in spring[^3]

**Additional Picnic Sites:** Picnic sites available in all recreation areas (no shelter)[^1]

#### **Flatwoods Monster Museum \& Braxton County Visitors Center**

- **Location:** 208 Main Street, downtown Sutton, WV 26601 (2 miles from I-79 Exit 62)[^14][^15]
- **Hours:** Tuesday-Friday 9 AM-5 PM; Weekends 10 AM-4 PM; Closed Mondays[^14]
- **Admission:** FREE[^14]
- **Description:** Museum features one-of-a-kind collections, historic items, memorabilia, books, and local cryptid exhibits; also serves as Braxton County Visitors Center with area brochures and travel information[^15][^14]
- **Note:** This is a county/local facility, not USACE-operated

### Ranger Programs and Educational Activities

**No scheduled ranger programs, guided tours, or educational activities at Sutton Lake are documented in official USACE sources.**

**Educational Features Available:**

- **Native Pollinator Garden at Sutton Dam:** Interpretive signage explaining importance of native plants for pollinators, insects, birds, and wildlife[^16][^17][^18]

### Fishing Piers

**Downstream Day Use Area:** Fishing pier available[^2][^3]

**Accessibility:** The fishing pier is located in an area with accessible flush toilets and parking, though specific ADA compliance details for the pier itself are not documented.[^2][^3]

### ADA Accessible Features

**Picnic Shelters:**

- **Shelter 1** (Downstream Area): ADA accessible[^3][^2]
- **Shelter 4** (Bee Run Area): ADA accessible, but no direct ADA access to restroom facilities[^2]

**Campground Facilities (Gerald R. Freeman):**[^11]

- Accessible boat ramps
- Accessible flush toilets
- Accessible parking
- Accessible picnic shelters
- Accessible playgrounds

**Campground Facilities (Bakers Run):**[^8]

- Accessible boat ramps
- Accessible parking
- Accessible drinking water
- Accessible flush toilets
- Accessible grills
- Accessible playgrounds
- Accessible showers

**Trails:**

- **Laurel Fork Trail** at nearby Holly River State Park (12-15 miles away): 0.4-mile ADA-accessible asphalt paved trail with rest stations, shelters, water fountains, and 10 historical markers with Braille signs[^20]
