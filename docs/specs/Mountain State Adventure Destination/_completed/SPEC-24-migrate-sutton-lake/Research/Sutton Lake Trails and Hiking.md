## Sutton Lake Trails and Hiking

### USACE Sutton Lake Trails

Sutton Lake offers **31 trail systems** for public use, with **over 30 miles** of trails on the Elk River Wildlife Management Area maintained by the West Virginia Department of Natural Resources. These include 22 additional access trails for hunters and fishermen located on the Wildlife Management Area.[^1][^2]

Many trails are located near Sutton Dam, with additional hiking trails in the Gerald R. Freeman Campground area.[^2][^3]

***

### Named Trails at Sutton Lake (Verified)

The following trails are officially documented by USACE:[^2]

#### **Jeremiah Carpenter Trail**

- **Trail Marker Color:** Lime Green
- **Distance:** 4.5 miles[^2]
- **Difficulty:** Not officially rated; described as featuring challenging terrain with a small river crossing[^2]
- **Description:** Begins downstream opposite the Sutton Lake Project office and travels up to the Bee Run campground entrance road. Offers views of the dam from the north abutment.[^4][^2]
- **Trailhead:** Downstream area opposite the dam; limited parking available at trailhead off Bee Run Road[^5]
- **Surface Type:** Not specified
- **Accessibility:** Not accessible


#### **Happy Camper's Trail**

- **Trail Marker Color:** Yellow
- **Distance:** 1.4 miles[^2]
- **Difficulty:** Not found
- **Trailhead:** Not found
- **Surface Type:** Not found
- **Accessibility:** Not found


#### **South Abutment Trail**

- **Trail Marker Color:** Purple
- **Distance:** 0.24 miles[^2]
- **Difficulty:** Easy (based on short distance)
- **Description:** Beautiful hike along the lake and rocks with views of Bee Run Marina[^2]
- **Trailhead:** South Abutment Day Use Area (behind the dam)[^2]
- **Surface Type:** Not found
- **Accessibility:** Not found


#### **Hardwood Timber Loop Trail**

- **Distance:** Not found
- **Difficulty:** Not found
- **Description:** Comprised of multiple connecting trails offering views of hardwood and softwood timber, with special planting areas for American Chestnut trees[^2]
- **Trailhead:** Near Sutton Dam[^6][^2]
- **Surface Type:** Not found
- **Accessibility:** Not found


#### **Sunrise Ridge Trail**

- **Distance:** Not found
- **Difficulty:** More arduous[^2]
- **Description:** Developed in 2019 with assistance from Glenville State College Forestry class and local volunteers. Offers scenic views of the Sutton Lake area[^2]
- **Trailhead:** Near Sutton Dam; same trailhead as Hardwood Timber Loop, Ellison Cemetery, Bug Ridge, and Sunshine Line trails[^6]
- **Surface Type:** Not found
- **Accessibility:** Not found


#### **Additional Trails Near Dam**

The following trails share a trailhead near Sutton Dam:[^6]

- **Ellison Cemetery Trail** (distance/difficulty not found)

### Trail Features and Policies

**Trail Marking System:**

- Each trail is marked with a specific color for directional guidance[^2]
- Trail markers display total distance traveled for ingress and egress[^2]

**General Recreation Policies:**

- Bicycles permitted on main roads and campground areas; contact Park Attendant or Ranger for off-road bicycling locations[^1]
- Horseback riding permitted in non-recreation areas with special regulations in WV DNR-leased areas[^1]
- Fire roads and hunter trails provide foot access to remote areas around Sutton Lake and Elk River Wildlife Management Area[^1]

**Additional Features:**

- Pollinator garden featuring West Virginia native plant species located near Sutton Dam[^6][^2]
- Trail maintenance performed by volunteers and USACE staff[^2]

**Trail Volunteer Opportunities:** Contact Sutton Lake Project Office at (304) 765-2816[^2]

### Nearby Trails (Within 15 Miles)

#### **Holly River State Park**

- **Location:** Webster County, approximately 12-15 miles from Sutton Lake[^7][^8]
- **Total Trail Mileage:** 42 miles[^9][^8]
- **Trail Types:** Hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails[^8]

**Easy Trails:**

**Laurel Fork Trail – 0.4 Miles**[^9]

- **Surface:** Asphalt paved
- **Accessibility:** ADA-accessible with four rest stations, two shelters, three water fountains, and 10 historical markers with Braille signs
- **Trailhead:** Activity Building/Carpenter Pavilion island area
- **Description:** Loop trail encircling the island along Laurel Fork

**Nature's Rock Garden Trail – 0.4 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Trailhead:** Cabin area parking lot
- **Description:** Self-guided interpretive loop with diverse plant life

**Salt Lick Trail – 0.75 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Trailhead:** Cabin No. 9
- **Description:** Winds through young forest to oldest trees in park, descends to picnic island

**Moderate Trails:**

**High Rock Trail – 1.5 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate (steep climb)
- **Trailhead:** Footbridge below Cabin No. 9
- **Description:** Steep climb to high tabletop rock on ridge summit

**Reverie Trail – 3.5 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Trailhead:** Campground entrance
- **Description:** Parallels Big Run, passes under Tecumseh Falls to primitive camping site

**Ridge Road Trail – 6.1 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Surface:** Jeep service road suitable for bicycling, day hiking, cross-country skiing, and horseback riding
- **Trailhead:** Laurel Fork near Jacob Klee Road
- **Description:** Follows ridge from old fire tower to campground

**Tramontane Trail – 2.5 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Trailhead:** Cabin No. 9
- **Features:** Mystic Falls at approximately 2.3 miles (hidden waterfall)
- **Description:** Winds through forest to old homestead fields, high ridge overlook

**Moderate to Difficult Trails:**

**Oak Ridge Trail – 2.5 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Difficult
- **Trailhead:** Lower end of campground
- **Features:** Overlook with park views

**Potato Knob Trail – 7.5 Miles (one way)**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Difficult
- **Elevation:** Summit at 2,480 feet
- **Trailhead:** Branches from Wilderness Trail
- **Features:** View of Tenskwatawa Falls, steep ascent to summit

**Railroad Grade Trail – 10 Miles**[^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Difficult
- **Trailhead:** Where Wilderness Trail crosses Crooked Fork
- **Description:** Follows old railroad grade through interesting rock formations

**Wilderness Trail – 4.5 Miles**[^10][^9]

- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Difficult
- **Trailhead:** Cabin No. 9
- **Features:** Old-growth forest areas
- **Description:** Ascends through high country glens to former fire tower site

**Holly River State Park Contact:** (304) 493-6353[^7]

***

#### **North Bend Rail Trail**

- **Total Distance:** 72 miles[^11][^12]
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate
- **Surface:** Crushed stone (double-track)[^13]
- **Route:** Wolf Summit (west of Clarksburg) to I-77 (near Parkersburg), running parallel to U.S. Route 50[^12][^11]
- **Features:** 36 bridges and 10 tunnels[^14]
- **Counties:** Harrison, Doddridge, Ritchie, and Wood[^11]
- **Towns Along Route:** Salem, West Union, Pennsboro, Ellenboro, Cairo, and North Bend State Park[^11]
- **Note:** The trail does not pass directly through Sutton; closest access points would be in Harrison County (eastern section), approximately 20-30 miles from Sutton Lake
