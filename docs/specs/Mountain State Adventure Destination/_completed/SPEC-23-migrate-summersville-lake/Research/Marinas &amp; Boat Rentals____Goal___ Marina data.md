# Summersville Lake Marinas \& Boat Rentals: 2024-2025 Guide

Summersville Lake, West Virginia's largest lake with 2,700 acres and over 60 miles of shoreline, offers comprehensive boating and water recreation services through a primary full-service marina and several secondary rental operators. This guide provides current contact information, rates, regulations, and practical visitor information for your LakeTemplate project.

## Primary Marina: Summersville Lake Marina

### Contact \& Hours

**Summersville Lake Marina** serves as the primary commercial marina facility on the lake. The facility is located at:[^1][^2][^3]

**Address:** 1706 Summersville Airport Rd, Summersville, WV 26651[^4][^5][^1]

**Phone:** (304) 872-1331[^5][^3][^1]

**Website:** summersvillelakemarina.com[^2]

**Hours of Operation:**

- Monday-Sunday: 7:00 AM - 11:00 PM[^3]
- **Operating Season:** Mid-May through mid-September (2025: May 15 - September 14)[^6][^4]

The marina is West Virginia's largest, with 473 boat slips and accommodations for seasonal and transient vessels.[^7]

### Boat Slip Rentals

**Seasonal Slips:** Available from mid-April through mid-September, subject to water level conditions. A lottery system is used for 2025 seasonal slip allocation. The marina operates on an annual lottery basis to ensure fair distribution among boat owners seeking extended mooring.[^1]

**Overnight Slips:** Available for \$25 per night plus 6% West Virginia sales tax.[^8]

**Transient Slip Partnerships:** The marina has partnered with Dockwa for convenient transient slip reservations online.[^2]

### Boat Rentals

**Fleet Composition:**

- 10 pontoon boats (24' x 8' each, capacity 12 passengers)[^3]
- 3 Triumph boats (17' length, capacity 4 passengers each)[^8][^3]

**Rental Rates (2025 Season):**

- **Monday-Thursday:** \$399 + taxes/fees + fuel[^6]
- **Friday \& Sunday \& Federal Holidays:** \$419 + taxes/fees + fuel[^6]
- **Saturday:** \$439 + taxes/fees + fuel[^6]

**Fuel Costs:** Average \$20-\$50 per rental; boats depart with full tanks[^8][^6]

**Payment Methods:** Credit/debit cards (all major processors), Apple Pay, Google Pay, cash (\$25 discount available)[^6]

**Rental Duration \& Deposit:**

- Check-in: 7:00 AM - 12:00 PM; Return by 7:00 PM[^6]
- 10% deposit charged at booking (credited toward final price)[^6]
- Remainder due after rental completion[^6]

**Capacity Limits:**

- Up to 12 passengers per pontoon (regardless of age)[^6]
- 1,700 lbs maximum weight (including gear)[^6]

**Amenities Included:**

- Bimini tops for shade
- US Coast Guard-approved life jackets
- Boarding ladder
- Pet-friendly accommodation
- Bluetooth and FM stereo systems[^6]

**Cancellation Policy:** Full refund of 10% deposit if cancelled by 11:00 PM the day before reservation[^6]

**Restrictions:** No alcohol, grills, rock-climbing gear, towing of tubes/water skiers, or anchors provided[^6]

**Operator Requirements:**

- Minimum age 21 years
- Valid driver's license and boater safety card (operators born Dec 31, 1986 or later)[^8][^6]
- Temporary boater safety cards available onsite
- Security deposit: \$1,000 on credit card for damage potential[^9]


### Ship Store \& Amenities

The marina offers ice, Pepsi beverages, ice cream, snacks, sunscreen, towels, and basic boating supplies. **Note:** Currently no restaurant at the marina; visitors should plan meals and beverages accordingly.[^4][^6]

**Fuel Dock:** Available with gasoline service. Diesel availability not specified in current documentation.[^4]

    - \$5 day-use fee per vehicle

## Boat Regulations \& Requirements

### Registration \& Horsepower

**Motorboat Registration Requirements:**

- Vessels 3+ horsepower require registration with West Virginia Division of Motor Vehicles[^17][^18]
- No registration fee for motors less than 3 horsepower or electric motors under 70 pounds thrust[^18][^17]
- Registration fees (3-year period): \$30 (Class A under 16'), \$45 (Class 1: 16-26'), \$60 (Class 2: 26-40'), \$75 (Class 3: 40'+)[^17]

**Age Restrictions on Operation:**

- Children 12-14 years may operate vessels powered by 10 hp or less if accompanied by person 18+ years old[^19][^20]
- Vessels 16 feet or longer require at least one Type IV (throwable) flotation device[^19]

**No Horsepower Restrictions on Summersville Lake:** Unlike some West Virginia waters, Summersville Lake does not appear to have horsepower limitations beyond manufacturer specifications. However, motorboats must observe marked no-wake zones.[^21][^22]

### Speed \& No-Wake Zones

**Regulated No-Wake Zones:**

- Marked by buoys or posted signs on the lake
- Beyond Route 19 bridge crossing (marked area)[^23]
- Operators must maintain no-wake speed (idling speed) within designated zones[^23][^19]

**General Speed Regulations:**

- Operators cannot exceed safe stopping distance[^19]
- Prohibited to become airborne within 100 feet of another boat[^19]
- Cannot follow another vessel closer than 10 mph within 100 feet rear and 50 feet side[^19]
- Cannot operate within 100 feet of swimmers, anchored boats, or human-powered craft at greater than idle speed[^19]


### Safety Equipment Requirements

**Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs):**

- All passengers must have properly sized, US Coast Guard-approved PFDs available[^9][^6]
- Operators of personal watercraft (PWC) and towed passengers must wear Type I, II, III, or V PFDs (inflatable types excluded)[^19]
- Life jackets provided with all rental boats[^6]

**Boater Safety Certification:**

- Operators born December 31, 1986 or after must possess valid boater safety card[^8][^6]
- Cards obtained through BoatUS Foundation or temporary cards issued by rental operators[^6]


### Invasive Species Inspection

West Virginia does not currently operate mandatory pre-launch watercraft inspection programs like some other states. However, boaters should practice clean, drain, dry protocols:[^24][^25][^26]

- Clean all visible aquatic plants and organisms from boat, trailer, and equipment before leaving any waterbody[^27]
- Drain all water from bilge, livewell, baitwell, and portable containers[^27]
- Remove drain plugs while transporting to prevent aquatic organism transport[^27]
- Live bait disposal in trash only (never release to waterbodies)[^27]

## Private Dock Permits

**USACE Regulations:** Private boat docks on Army Corps of Engineers-managed reservoirs require Special Use Permits (SUP) from the Huntington District office. Key restrictions include:[^30][^31]

- **No Commercial Use:** Private docks cannot be used for commercial purposes or rental to third parties[^32]
- **Adjacent Property Only:** Permits issued only to adjacent waterfront property owners[^33][^31]
- **Permit Compliance:** Dock design, construction, and maintenance must meet USACE shoreline management standards[^31]
- **Enforcement:** Violation can result in permit revocation (no replacement issued if lost)[^32]

Contact information for permits: **US Army Corps of Engineers Huntington District, Summersville Lake Office: (304) 872-3412 or pa2@usace.army.mil**[^34]

## Houseboat Rentals

**Summersville Lake Status:** Houseboats are **not currently available for rental at Summersville Lake**.[^35][^14]

**Alternative:** **Sutton Lake Marina** (nearby Sutton, WV) offers houseboats for 3-7 day rentals. Current houseboat rental offerings at other West Virginia lakes provide full accommodation capabilities for multi-day family vacations, though specific Summersville Lake houseboat services have not been developed.[^15][^36]

## 2024-2025 Seasonal Overview

**Operating Season:** Mid-May through mid-September annually. Lake level management by USACE affects exact opening/closing dates:

- **Summer Pool Level:** 1,652 feet elevation (creates 2,790 surface acres)[^34][^7]
- **Winter Drawdown:** Begins in late fall; lake lowered to 1,575 feet elevation for flood storage[^34][^7]
- **Camping Dates:** Battle Run Campground opens May 1, closes Columbus Day[^7]
- **Boat Rental Seasons:** Most commercial operators: Mid-May to mid-September[^4][^6]
