## Sutton Lake Fishing

### Fish Species Present

**Lake Species:**[^1][^2][^3]

- **Bass:** Largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, spotted bass
- **Crappie:** Black crappie, white crappie
- **Catfish:** Channel catfish, flathead catfish, blue catfish
- **Sunfish:** Bluegill, other sunfish species
- **Walleye**

**Tailwaters (Elk River below dam):**[^4][^5][^6][^3][^7][^1]

- **Muskie** (muskellunge)
- **Walleye**
- **Trout:** Rainbow, golden rainbow, brook, brown, and tiger trout (stocked)
- **Bass:** Largemouth and smallmouth bass
- **Catfish**
- **Panfish**


### Known Fishing Spots

The WV DNR fishing map shows depth contours at 20-foot intervals and identifies the following public access locations:[^8]

**Public Fishing Access Sites (4 boat ramps):**

- Bee Run Day Use Area
- South Abutment (behind dam)
- Bakers Run Campground
- Gerald R. Freeman Campground

**General Fishing Areas:**

- Lake extends 14 miles along the Elk River with many coves along 40 miles of shoreline[^9]
- Depth contours range from 20 to 100+ feet at various locations throughout the lake[^8]
- The lake's numerous coves provide habitat for bass and crappie[^2]
- Laydowns and structure are key targets, especially in springtime for bass[^10]

**Specific named fishing locations are not found in official sources.** The DNR fishing map provides depth contours and access points but does not label specific coves, points, or fishing spots by name.[^8]

### Seasonal Patterns

**Spring:**

- Bass fishing productive around laydowns and structure[^10]
- Trout stocking in tailwaters occurs bi-weekly February–April, with one additional stocking in May[^11][^7]
- Spring considered a prime fishing season with moderate temperatures and increased fish activity[^12]

**Summer:**

- Lake level maintained at summer pool elevation (922 feet MSL)
- Warm-water species (bass, catfish, crappie) active
- Lake described as "laid-back" compared to busier WV lakes[^7]

**Fall:**

- Trout stocked in tailwaters during fall season[^5][^6]
- Prime fishing season with moderate temperatures[^12]
- Fall fishing considered excellent

**Winter:**

- Lake drawn down to winter pool elevation (895 feet MSL)
- Cold-water fishing opportunities in tailwaters for trout
- Muskie and walleye fishing in tailwaters can be productive[^4]


### Stocking Programs (WV DNR)

**Trout Stocking - Sutton Lake Tailwaters (Elk River below dam):**[^11]

**Code: BW-F** (Braxton County)

- **February–April:** Two stockings per month
- **January:** One stocking
- **May:** One stocking
- **October:** One stocking each week for two weeks, beginning on the third Monday of October

**Species Stocked:** Rainbow trout, golden rainbow trout, brook trout, brown trout, and tiger trout[^7][^11]

**No lake stocking programs are documented for Sutton Lake itself** – only the tailwaters receive trout stockings.[^11]

**Stocking Hotline:** (304) 558-3399 for daily updates during trout stocking season[^11]

### Fishing Tournaments

**Annual Tournaments:**

**WV BASS Nation:**

- Sutton Lake hosts annual State Qualifying Tournament (SQT) at Marina ramp[^13]
- Scheduled date: September 26 (Southeastern Region)[^13]
- Also scheduled for April 4 tournament[^13]

**Student Angler Federation (SAF):**

- WV SAF State Championship held annually at Sutton Lake[^14]
- Location: Bee Run boat ramp, Sutton, WV
- Scheduled date: April 12, 2026[^14]

**Other Tournaments:**

- Sutton Lake is a popular location for bass tournaments[^2]
- Four crappie tournaments held in 2021 at Sutton Lake with 109 crappie reported[^15]
- Lake ranked 6th in WV for tournament activity in 2021 with catch-rate of 0.26 fish/hour[^16]
- Multiple regional bass tournaments scheduled throughout the year[^17][^18]


### Special Regulations

**Walleye - Slot Limit (Sutton Lake and Elk River including Sutton Lake):**[^19][^20][^21][^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 2 walleye
- **Slot limit:** All walleye 20-30 inches must be immediately released
- **Harvest:** Only walleye under 20 inches OR one walleye over 30 inches may be kept
- **Possession limit:** 16 (statewide)

**Black Bass:**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 6 fish (smallmouth, largemouth, spotted in any combination)
- **Minimum size:** None
- **Possession limit:** 12

**Channel Catfish:**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 30 (USACE lakes and rivers)
- **Minimum size:** None
- **Possession limit:** 60

**Blue Catfish:**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 2
- **Minimum size:** 25 inches
- **Possession limit:** No limit

**Flathead Catfish:**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 30
- **Minimum size:** None
- **Possession limit:** 60

**Crappie (Black and White in aggregate):**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 30
- **Minimum size:** None
- **Possession limit:** 60

**Muskellunge:**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 1
- **Minimum size:** 30 inches
- **Possession limit:** 2

**Trout (Tailwaters):**[^11]

- **Daily creel limit:** 6 (all species in combination)
- **Minimum size:** None
- **Possession limit:** 12

**Additional Restrictions:**

- Spearfishing prohibited on Elk River from Sutton Dam downstream to US Route 19 bridge at Sutton[^21]
- Droplines prohibited on Elk River[^21]
- **Fish consumption advisory:** Black bass over 12 inches limited to 1 meal per month due to mercury[^11]
