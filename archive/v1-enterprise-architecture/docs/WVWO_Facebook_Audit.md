## Comparison: Facebook vs. Website Strategy

### Facebook Should Handle:
- ✅ **Real-time stock alerts** (".270 ammo just arrived - call before you drive")
- ✅ **Same-day announcements** ("Open Sunday after church 1-5pm")
- ✅ **Community engagement** (respond to comments, share customer photos)
- ✅ **Amplify website content** (share blog posts, drive traffic)

### Facebook Should NOT Handle:
- ❌ **Primary hours/contact info** → Website is source of truth
- ❌ **Brand narrative/flood story** → Website tells full story
- ❌ **SEO/discoverability** → Website ranks for "gun shop Braxton County WV"
- ❌ **Product catalog** → Website has permanent, searchable inventory
- ❌ **Trust building** → Website has reviews, credentials, full story

## Key Takeaway

**Current State:** Facebook page exists but doesn't work. It's a dormant product announcement board with no community, personality, or strategy.

**Root Cause:** Treated as obligation ("we should post") rather than tool ("this helps us sell").

**Path Forward:**
1. **Phase 1:** Website becomes hub (hours, story, SEO, email signup)
2. **Phase 2:** Facebook becomes spoke (real-time alerts, community, drive to website)
3. **Result:** Each platform does what it's good at, together they drive foot traffic

**Bottom Line:** Don't try to "fix" Facebook. Build website first, then use Facebook strategically to amplify it.
